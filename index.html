Why the faces are blank. Every portrait here is an original plaster relief, not a still — a mask on the front of the cast, a bare face on the back. The actual reveal lives one click away on each specimen's back panel. That's on purpose: turning the cast over to find the human is the whole point, and it lands harder than a thumbnail.

Fork the wall. Every specimen is one object in a plain SPECIMENS array at the top of the script. Add a row — character, actor, production, the designer credit, a transformation rating, a "known for," and one good reveal — and it appears. No build step. The benchmark is the schema: no entry without a designed face behind it.
